Setting up an App Pair on your Samsung tablet is a game-changer for field work. It allows you to launch Spck and Termux simultaneously with one swipe, making your data entry and document building feel like a single, seamless application.

🛠️ How to Create the App Pair
Open both apps: Open Spck Editor first, then open Termux.

Enter Split Screen: * Tap the Recents button (the three vertical lines in your nav bar).

Tap the icon at the top of the Spck window and select "Open in split screen view."

Select Termux as the second app.

Save the Pair:

In the center divider between the two apps, tap the three dots (or the star icon if visible).

Tap the Add to Edge Panel icon (it looks like a plus sign inside a side panel).

Now, whenever you pull out your Edge Panel (the transparent handle on the side of your screen), you’ll see an icon for Spck/Termux together. One tap, and your field lab is open.

📂 Accessing Files in Termux (The "Sync" Bridge)
There is one technical hurdle: Spck stores files in its own "sandbox" which Termux can't see by default. To bridge this:

In Termux: Run termux-setup-storage.

In Spck: When you clone your repo, choose a location inside your internal storage (like a folder named Documents/Geology) rather than the default internal Spck folder.

The Result: Now, when you edit the JSON in Spck and save, Termux is looking at the exact same file. You won't have to copy or move anything.

🧗 The Final "Summit" View
You are now equipped with:

The Brain: index.js (The logic).

The Body: travelData.json (The data).

The Tools: Spck (The UI/Git) and Termux (The Engine).

The Workflow: App Pairs for speed.
